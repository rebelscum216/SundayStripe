I did a real crawl/search pass on SundayStripe.com, and the good news is Google is already discovering your homepage, collections, product pages, About page, Contact page, the golf stands page, and topical landing pages like “Funny Golf Gloves.”

My overall take: the site is indexable and has solid raw product inventory, but it is under-leveraging SEO because a lot of pages are thin, repetitive, or too generic. The biggest upside is not some obscure technical trick. It is better keyword targeting, stronger category/page copy, better trust content, and cleaning up template noise so the pages read more clearly to both Google and humans.

What is working

Your site has multiple crawlable keyword entry points already. Google can see collections for apparel, hoodies, gloves, and topical pages like “Funny Golf Gloves,” plus individual products with descriptive titles like the Azalea shirt and the new SEND IT glove. That is a good foundation because you are not starting from zero.

Some of the product copy is also actually pretty decent. The SEND IT glove page has real benefit-based bullets, and the Azalea tee snippet has descriptive, golf-relevant language. Pages like that are much stronger SEO assets than generic print-on-demand style listings.

You also have a legit local angle. The golf stands page explicitly says pickup or local delivery in the Cleveland area, and your About page/search snippet ties the brand to Cleveland. That gives you an opening for local-intent pages and regional search visibility that most generic apparel stores do not have.

Biggest SEO problems

The homepage looks weak from an SEO perspective. In the crawlable HTML, the homepage mostly exposes banners, product cards, and very little rich explanatory copy beyond things like “Skull & Tees Golf Gloves,” “Skull & Tees Hooded Shirt,” and “New Releases.” The page title showing in search is simply “Sunday Stripe Golf,” which is brand-forward but not very keyword-rich for non-branded discovery.

Your collection pages are indexable, but the messaging is not consistent enough. For example, the /collections/golf-apparel page is titled “Sunday Stripe Golf T-Shirts & Hoodies,” while the on-page H1 is “Collection: Sunday Stripe Graphic Tees.” That mismatch makes the page less focused than it could be. It also reads more like a generic Shopify collection than a deliberately optimized landing page.

There is a lot of template clutter in the crawlable HTML. On multiple pages, Google can see very long country/region selectors, duplicated navigation blocks, repeated product names, repeated headings, repeated prices, cart text, and boilerplate like “Item added to your cart.” That does not necessarily break SEO, but it dilutes topical clarity and makes thin pages look even thinner.

Some product pages are much too thin. The Cuyahoga sweater page, for example, is basically title, price, sold-out status, materials, and the line “For JJeff,” followed by generic fulfillment text. That is nowhere near as strong as the better glove/shirt pages and gives Google little unique context to rank on.

Your trust pages are also thin. The About page is only a sentence plus “Andrew Smith, Founder and designer,” and the Contact page is essentially just a form. That is a missed opportunity for both trust and SEO.

One more issue: your About page search snippet includes “We are not associated with any political party or candidate.” That may be accurate for brand protection, but in search results it feels out of place and can confuse people who are just trying to buy golf apparel.

Highest-impact SEO moves
1) Rewrite the homepage around search intent

Right now the homepage feels like a storefront first and a landing page second. You need a tighter intro block near the top that clearly says what Sunday Stripe is and who it is for.

A stronger version would target phrases like:

funny golf gloves
golf graphic tees
golf hoodies
Cleveland golf apparel
unique golf gifts

That copy should not be stuffed. Just make it obvious that Sunday Stripe sells funny and distinctive golf gloves, graphic tees, hoodies, and locally made/custom golf stands. Those themes are already present across your site, but they are not concentrated well on the homepage.

A better homepage title could be something like:
Funny Golf Gloves, Graphic Tees & Golf Hoodies | Sunday Stripe

A better homepage meta description could be:
Shop Sunday Stripe for funny golf gloves, graphic golf tees, golf hoodies, and custom golf stands. Cleveland-designed golf gear for the course and the couch.

2) Turn collections into true landing pages

Your collection pages already exist, which is great. Now they need 150–300 words of real copy each, above or below the product grid.

Best opportunities I see:

Golf gloves
Graphic tees / golf apparel
Hoodies & sweatshirts
Funny golf gloves
Custom golf stands
Cleveland golf gear / Cleveland collection

You already have signals for these topics on the site, especially gloves, apparel, funny golf gloves, and Cleveland/local stands.

For example, your golf gloves collection could open with a short paragraph like:
“Shop premium golf gloves designed to stand out on the course. Sunday Stripe golf gloves combine comfort, grip, and bold personality with designs made for golfers who want more than basic white gear.”

That is the kind of copy that helps ranking and conversion at the same time.

3) Standardize product page quality

You have inconsistent product-detail depth. Some are strong, some are almost empty. Every product page should have:

a 1–2 paragraph product intro
3–6 benefit bullets
material/fit details
who it is for
occasion/gift angle
internal links to the parent collection or related products

The gap between your SEND IT glove page and the Cuyahoga sweater page shows exactly why this matters.

A simple product-page structure that would help:

Opening paragraph with keyword + vibe
“Why golfers like it”
Sizing/materials
“Pairs well with” or “Shop more gloves / tees / hoodies”
4) Build a few non-product SEO pages

You need more content that can rank without requiring a person to be ready to buy one exact SKU.

The best pages for you:

Best funny golf gloves
Golf gift ideas for men
What makes a good golf glove
Cleveland golf gifts
Custom golf bag stands in Cleveland
Best golf graphic tees for casual rounds / outings

You already have enough products and positioning to support these topics naturally. The “Funny Golf Gloves” page is proof that this direction fits your brand.

These pages should link into your actual products and collections, not just exist as blog filler.

5) Expand the About and Contact pages for trust

Your About page needs a real founder story, brand angle, and Cleveland tie-in. The current version is too short to build much trust. Your Contact page should add at least an email address, expected response time, maybe a short FAQ, and for the golf stands side, local-service language.

I would add:

why you started Sunday Stripe
what makes the designs different
where you are based
whether products are designed in Cleveland
what is made to order vs stocked
shipping/returns summary
custom inquiries for golf stands
General website notes beyond SEO

The brand positioning is clear enough stylistically, but the site still feels a bit like a default Shopify catalog in places. The crawlable content shows repeated headings, repeated product titles, and lots of generic commerce boilerplate. That makes the store feel less “brand-built” than it could.

Sold-out handling could also be better. Several pages show sold-out products, and in one case the new SEND IT glove is sold out on-site while also pointing users to Amazon. That can be fine, but it should be intentional. If Amazon is your backup conversion path, frame it clearly. If not, too many sold-out pages can make the store feel stale.

The golf stands page is actually one of the more promising business pages because it has specificity: pricing tiers, configurations, Cleveland-only delivery/pickup, and customization. That page is closer to a service landing page than a normal Shopify page, and you should treat it like one. It could probably win local search traffic faster than the apparel side if you lean into custom golf bag stand keywords and Cleveland-area intent.

Specific fixes I’d do first

First, rewrite the homepage title, meta description, and intro copy so it can rank for non-branded terms.

Second, add keyword-rich intro copy to every main collection.

Third, upgrade all thin product pages, starting with your best sellers and anything seasonal.

Fourth, turn the About page into an actual brand page and the Contact page into a trust page.

Fifth, create 3–5 search-targeted evergreen content pages around golf gifts, funny golf gloves, and Cleveland golf products.

Sixth, review the theme and trim any unnecessary crawlable clutter where possible, especially duplicated headings and excessive template boilerplate. The repeated nav/country blocks and duplicate product headings are visible in the HTML Google can crawl.
